The account question

No login.
No cloud profile.
No trade.

For this local-first release, Kerna does not need signup. You initialize a workspace on your machine, bring the model provider you choose, and retain the ability to inspect or remove your local data.

What this means in practice

The product works without becoming another identity system.

Avoiding accounts removes a first-run barrier and respects the product’s trust posture. It also means user count needs an honest definition.

NO
ACCOUNT
REQUIRED
01 / INSTALL COUNTS

Useful, but not users.

GitHub Release download totals and npm’s aggregate package-download reports tell us that artifacts were fetched. They cannot distinguish one person reinstalling from ten people trying Kerna, nor prove that anyone completed a useful task.

02 / ACTIVE USE

Measure with consent.

If Kerna later adds telemetry, it should be opt-in, anonymous, and content-free: product version, platform, a rotating random install identifier, and outcome counters such as first receipt completed. Never send goals, tool arguments, task traces, tokens, or workspace contents.

03 / COHORT EVIDENCE

Learn from real outcomes.

For the first cohort, the reliable signal is a voluntary acceptance record: whether each participant reached a useful local result in 15 minutes, understood approvals, and reported a privacy or policy issue.

04 / FUTURE ACCOUNTS

Add only for a clear job.

A future shared-policy, team governance, or hosted-connector experience may need identity. That is a separate product decision with a clear data model, not a default requirement for personal Kerna use.

LOCAL-FIRST BY DEFAULT

A better north star

Optimize for useful, safe outcomes, not raw tool calls.

Track activation through a first useful receipt, retention through recurring reviewed routines, and trust through clear approvals and zero unapproved external side effects. None of those require reading a person’s work.
